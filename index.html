History of the School

Established in 2008, our school began with a humble vision of providing quality education in a caring and disciplined environment. We opened our doors with just eight pupils, driven by a commitment to nurture young minds and lay a solid academic and moral foundation for every child entrusted to our care. Through dedication, consistency, and the support of parents and the community, the school has grown steadily over the years. From those modest beginnings, we have expanded our enrolment, improved our facilities, and strengthened our academic programmes to meet the evolving needs of our learners. Since inception, we have successfully graduated pupils who have progressed to reputable secondary schools, where many have continued to excel academically and socially. Their achievements reflect our unwavering commitment to excellence in teaching, character development, and holistic education. At our school, we believe that every child has unique potential. We therefore provide a nurturing environment that promotes academic excellence, moral integrity, creativity, leadership, and self-confidence. Our dedicated and qualified staff work tirelessly to ensure that each learner receives the guidance and support needed to thrive. Today, we remain committed to our founding vision of raising confident, responsible, and lifelong learners. As we continue to grow, we look forward to shaping future generations through quality education, strong values, and a passion for excellence.

Activities in the school

Sports & Physical Activity

Team Sports: Football, Basketball.
Individual Sports: swimming, gymnastics, and athletics,
Martial Arts: Taekwondo. (excellent for focus and self-discipline).
Dance: Ballet.

Creative & Performing Arts

Arts & Crafts: Painting, pottery, origami, sketching, and sculpting.
Music: Learning an instrument (recorder, piano, violin, guitar, or drums) and school choir.
Drama & Theater: Acting clubs, public speaking, and musical theater groups.
Photography: Basic digital photography and visual storytelling.

STEM & Academic Clubs

Designed to stimulate curiosity, critical thinking, and problem-solving.
Coding & Robotics: Basic block coding (like Scratch) and building simple Lego robots.
Chess & Board Games: Boosts strategic thinking and patience.
Science & Nature Club: Fun hands-on experiments, gardening, and learning about ecosystems.
Creative Writing & Book Club: Enhances literacy, imagination, and a love for reading.

Life Skills & Community

Focuses on leadership, social responsibility, and practical skills.
Scouting & Guiding: Boy Scouts, Girl Guides, or Brownies for outdoor skills, survival basics, and community service.
Cooking & Baking: Simple, age-appropriate culinary classes to teach kitchen safety and nutrition.
Debate & Public Speaking: Builds confidence and articulation in older primary students.
Eco-Club / Recycling: Initiatives centered around school gardening, composting, and environmental awareness.
